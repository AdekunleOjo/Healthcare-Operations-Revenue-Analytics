# 🏥 Healthcare Operations & Revenue Analytics

## 📊 Overview
This project presents an end-to-end healthcare analytics workflow using SQL Server and Power BI.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze patient trends
- Identify operational inefficiencies
- Evaluate financial performance

## 📂 Dataset
- Patients
- Visits
- Billing
- Medications

## 🧹 Data Cleaning
Performed using SQL Server:
- Standardized IDs
- Fixed date formats
- Cleaned missing values
- Corrected errors

## 📊 Dashboard
3-page Power BI dashboard:
- Executive Summary
- Operational Insights
- Financial Analysis

## 🔍 Key Insight
- Patient demand is steadily increasing over time, indicating growing healthcare utilization  
- Chronic conditions such as Diabetes and Hypertension are the primary drivers of visits across all departments  
- Operational performance is efficient, with a low average length of stay (~1.16 days) enabling high patient throughput  
- Only ~50% of total revenue is successfully collected, revealing significant revenue leakage  
- A large portion of revenue remains unpaid or pending, posing a major cash flow risk  
- Revenue contribution is relatively evenly distributed across insurance providers, reducing dependency risk  
- Middle-aged patient groups (31–60) account for the highest volume of visits  
- No single department dominates workload, suggesting balanced operational distribution  
- A small number of high-value patients contribute disproportionately to total revenue  
- A noticeable drop in revenue in early 2025 suggests potential data anomalies or operational disruptions
